About

I am starting my Master's degree in Language Technologies at Carnegie Mellon University this Fall. I received my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics & Computer Science with highest distinction from École Polytechnique where I defended my thesis on sign language translation.

For as long as I can remember, I've had a passion for mathematics. I actually learned to do multiplications and divisions before I learned to read! It was not until my undergraduate studies that I discovered and quickly became obsessed with programming. Music is also a life-long passion of mine, and also what introduced me to AI. My worlds collided when I used LSTMs to generate music for my second-year programming project .

I fell in love with NLP during my summer internship at Illuin Technology. Unwilling to let go of my project when my internship ended, I continued working as a freelance researcher for Illuin and researched unsupervised and French question answering. Then, I finally moved on to research sign language translation at LIX lab.

My name is pronounced [kajo iɴ].

Blog

In high school I loved creative writing assignments, and I used to run an art blog as well. Now, I can continue writing while both learning new ML topics and sharing my ideas by posting data science articles!

I am thrilled at all the great feedback my blog posts have received, at the wonderful LinkedIn connections I have made from them, and earning the badge "Top writer in Artificial Intelligence"

  • Dec. 2019 Overcome Overfitting During Instance Segmentation with Mask-RCNN

    I show how to train Mask-RCNN for instance segmentation on only 1,349 annotated images.
  • Dec. 2019 Step-by-Step Signal Processing with Machine Learning: Manifold Learning

    I present and explain how to implement Isomap and Locally Linear Embedding for dimensionality reduction.
  • Nov. 2019 How to Train StyleGAN to Generate Realistic Faces

    I introduce the StyleGAN architecture and its advantages for artificial face generation. I equally share a training and evaluation pipeline in PyTorch.
  • Nov. 2019 Step-by-Step Signal Processing with Machine Learning: PCA, ICA, NMF for source separation, dimensionality reduction

    I present and explain how to implement Principal Components Analysis for dimensionality reduction, as well as Independent Components Analysis and Nonnegative Matrix Factorization for source separation.
  • Oct. 2019 Latest Winning Techniques for Kaggle Image Classification with Limited Data

    I explain my approach in a multi-class image classification class challenge that allowed me to win second place. I present various methods such as data augmentation, anti-aliasing, transfer learning, snapshot ensembleing to overcome shortage of data and the danger of overfitting.
  • Oct. 2019 Unsupervised Question Answering

    I present an approach towards unsupervised question answering using Cloze Translation. I introduce the XLNet architecture for question answering and show how I obtained state-of-the-art results in unsupervised question answering with this model.
  • Oct. 2019 Making Convolutional Networks Shift-Invariant Again

    My debut article on Towards Data Science. I explain the paper with the same title, notably why modern CNN architectures are not shift-invariant and how we can reintroduce anti-aliasing.
  • Jul. 2019 Cleaning Up Dirty Scanned Documents with Deep Learning

    My grand data science blogging debut for the company blog of my summer internship. I explain two methods I developed for denoising of scanned text documents. The first method, based on image processing techniques and ensemble learning with CNN and XGBoost provides performance with a rank of 11th place for a Kaggle challenge. The second method uses CycleGAN that can train on data without parallel corpora of noisy and clean images.
